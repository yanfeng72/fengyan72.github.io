Research Publications Teaching Education

My research sits at the intersection of higher education policy and teaching and learning, with a focus on how decisions about course format, scheduling, and delivery modality shape student learning outcomes. I work primarily with large-scale administrative data and apply causal inference methods to questions that bear directly on institutional policy and student success. I have also collaborated with researchers in learning sciences and educational psychology, drawing on surveys, designed interventions, multimodal data, and interviews to examine how instructional and contextual factors shape student learning and development. My current work also examines how technology shapes teaching, learning, and student outcomes, including the design of AI-driven behavioral interventions to support college student success.

Current Projects

The Effects of Condensed Course Formats on Student Achievement

UCI · 2025–Present

Graduate Student Researcher · Ascendium Grant · PIs: Dr. Di Xu & Dr. XunFei Li

Analyze large-scale administrative data from the Virginia Community College System (VCCS) to estimate the causal effects of condensed course formats on students' academic and labor market outcomes using quasi-experimental methods, including two-way fixed effects and IV approaches. Conduct heterogeneity analyses by student subgroups, disciplines, and enrollment patterns.

Modality, Student Characteristics, and Academic Outcomes: A Study of Transfer Students in Undergraduate Business Administration

UCI · 2025–Present

Graduate Student Researcher · PERI Institute · PIs: Dr. Devin Shanthikumar, Dr. Di Xu, Dr. Sabrina Solanki

Evaluate a post-COVID undergraduate business administration program through the lens of course modality; analyze online vs. face-to-face enrollment patterns with a focus on transfer student sorting; developing causal identification strategy to estimate modality effects on student outcomes.

TimeWise: Time Management and Learning Behaviors in College STEM Courses

UCI · 2025–Present

Research Assistant · PIs: Dr. Di Xu & Dr. Qiujie Li

Developing a multi-construct survey instrument measuring time management and procrastination behaviors, adapting items from validated scales; coordinating data collection in introductory STEM courses; contributing to the design of a time management app and behavioral intervention for college students.
